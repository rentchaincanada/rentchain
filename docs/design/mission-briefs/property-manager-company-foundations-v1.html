Next Mission: feat/property-manager-company-foundations-v1

Backend foundations for Property Manager Companies, using Delegated Access V1 as the governance reference pattern: fail closed, preserve actor attribution, keep audit metadata append-safe, and avoid UI until backend foundations are stable.

Mission Goal

Implement backend-only model and helper foundations for Property Manager Companies so future UI and workflow missions can build on stable company identity, membership, role templates, landlord-company relationships, relationship status behavior, authorization evaluation, and audit event builders.

Scope

Company identity propertyManagerCompany with company name, status, createdByUserId, createdAt, updatedAt, and safe display label. Distinct from delegates and individual users. No public branding/profile.
Membership companyId, userId, role, status, invited/created by, createdAt, updatedAt, suspendedAt, and removedAt where applicable.
Role templates Company Owner, Company Admin, Regional Manager, Property Manager, Leasing Agent, Office Administrator, Maintenance Coordinator, and Read-only Staff.
Relationship foundation landlordId, propertyManagerCompanyId, status, property scope, workspace scope, lifecycle timestamps, accepting admin, and termination reason.

Relationship Status Behavior

Status Authorization Behavior History Behavior
pending Does not grant access. Relationship draft/history is preserved.
active Can grant scoped access when membership, role, workspace, and property checks pass. Lifecycle and evaluated scope remain auditable.
suspended Blocks access temporarily. Relationship and audit history are preserved for possible reactivation.
terminated Blocks access permanently unless a future reviewed reactivation process exists. Final relationship state and audit history are preserved.

Authorization Foundation

Helper functions only. No route integration unless needed for tests and explicitly reviewed.
  • Landlord remains data owner; PM company receives scoped operating authority only.
  • Keep company membership separate from landlord-company relationships.
  • Evaluate authenticated user.
  • Require active company membership.
  • Require active landlord-company relationship.
  • Apply predefined staff role/template.
  • Evaluate property scope: all-current-properties or selected-properties.
  • Evaluate workspace scope.
  • Fail closed on malformed, missing, pending, suspended, terminated, or out-of-scope state.
  • Keep billing/settings landlord-owner only.

Non-goals

No UI No frontend routes No dashboard No invite emails No staff assignment UI No contractor organizations No billing delegation No custom permissions No staff-to-staff delegation No migration/backfill No production route exposure

Audit Foundations

Add metadata-only event builders. They should produce stable event shapes without persisting secrets, broad payloads, raw provider data, or user-facing raw IDs.

Event builders
  • company created
  • membership created
  • membership suspended
  • membership removed
  • relationship created
  • relationship activated
More lifecycle events
  • relationship suspended
  • relationship reactivated
  • relationship terminated
  • staff assignment changed if modeled

Audit Shape

Required metadata actorUserId, actorCompanyId when applicable, actingForLandlordId, relationshipId, role, scope, target resource, outcome, timestamp.
Safety Metadata-only, projection-safe, no public labels built from raw internal IDs.

Role and Permission Rules

  • No custom roles.
  • No raw permission builder.
  • Use predefined templates only.
  • Company Owner/Admin manage staff; ordinary staff cannot invite or delegate to other staff.
  • Company staff cannot exceed landlord-company relationship scope.
  • Explicit deny wins over role/template permissions.
  • Suspended and terminated relationships fail closed.
  • Billing/settings remain landlord-owner only.
