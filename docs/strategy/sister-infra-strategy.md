# Sister Infrastructure Strategy (Non-Crypto)

## Positioning (One Page)
- Sister infrastructure is a separate reliability and interoperability layer for data movement, audit packaging, and partner connectors.
- It is not a token, chain, wallet, or financial product.
- Primary business purpose:
  - improve integration speed with external systems
  - standardize event exchange and evidence packaging
  - reduce operational friction for enterprise workflows
- Product posture:
  - utility infrastructure for platform operators
  - compliance-aware by design
  - independent from end-user credit or lease decisions

## Naming Risk and Options
## Risk
- Using names too close to the main product domain can create confusion in sales, legal copy, and partner due diligence.
- Avoid names that suggest:
  - consumer-facing lending
  - crypto assets
  - replacement of core compliance features

## Naming Options
- Option A: `BridgeCore`
  - Emphasizes interoperability and transport layer.
- Option B: `SignalFabric`
  - Emphasizes normalized event streams and reliability.
- Option C: `OpsRelay`
  - Emphasizes operational routing and automation support.
- Option D: `EvidenceMesh`
  - Emphasizes audit/evidence packaging across systems.

## Naming Guidance
- Keep the sister brand as a sub-brand with clear “infrastructure layer” descriptor.
- Do not use the same root domain naming as the primary product.
- Keep website and docs IA distinct to prevent accidental positioning overlap.

## Separation from Compliance Narrative
- Compliance remains anchored in the primary product experience and policy controls.
- Sister infrastructure should be described as:
  - a technical enabler for integrations and observability
  - not a legal interpretation or compliance authority
- Documentation boundaries:
  - primary product docs: compliance workflows, lifecycle controls, legal context
  - sister infra docs: adapters, transport contracts, reliability SLAs, audit packaging format
- GTM boundaries:
  - compliance claims stay with the core platform
  - sister infra claims stay with delivery reliability, integration depth, and operational governance
