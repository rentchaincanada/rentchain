# RentChain vs Yardi Positioning v1

Branch: `docs/enterprise-layer-roadmap-v1`
Scope: strategic positioning only; no marketing claim, implementation, or pricing change.

## Positioning Rule

RentChain should not claim that it replaces Yardi Voyager today.

Yardi Voyager is mature enterprise property management and accounting infrastructure. It has decades of depth across accounting, leasing, property operations, reporting, and enterprise workflows.

RentChain's enterprise strategy should focus on specific wedge features before attempting full replacement.

## What Yardi Represents

For enterprise landlords, Yardi Voyager represents:

- complex accounting
- portfolio operations
- lease administration
- reporting
- integrations
- permissions
- mature back-office workflows
- operational familiarity

The comparison sets a high bar. RentChain should treat it as enterprise context, not as a current parity target.

## RentChain's Potential Wedge

RentChain should compete by being:

- modern
- workflow-governed
- tenant-facing
- evidence-aware
- API-friendly
- faster to adopt
- easier to pilot
- clearer about consent, audit, and role boundaries

The wedge is not "all accounting and property management on day one." The wedge is governed operational workflows that large landlords need and legacy systems may not make easy.

## Strongest Wedge Areas

### Tenant-Facing Workflow

RentChain can differentiate through tenant portal experiences, consent-aware screening, lease workflow transparency, payment readiness, and tenant communication surfaces.

### Governance And Audit

RentChain can make actor attribution, delegated access, PM company authority, workflow evidence, and audit history first-class.

### API-Friendly Operations

A safe vacancy feed/API, enterprise API keys, and export-first accounting pathways can let RentChain coexist with existing systems before replacing them.

### Evidence-Aware Workflows

Evidence packages, lease lifecycle evidence, screening consent trails, payment authorization history, and compliance workflow records can become high-value enterprise differentiators.

### Adoption Speed

RentChain can offer focused workflows that are easier to pilot than a full enterprise platform migration.

## Areas Where RentChain Should Not Overclaim

RentChain should not currently claim:

- full Yardi Voyager replacement
- enterprise accounting parity
- automated PAD support
- automated screening product readiness
- vacancy API availability
- production-grade enterprise API access
- full multi-entity ownership hierarchy
- custom enterprise permission builder
- institutional/legal certification
- government submission readiness

## Recommended Market Language

Safe:

- "RentChain is building a governed enterprise layer for modern rental operations."
- "RentChain can complement existing property management systems through tenant-facing workflows, audit-safe operations, and API-friendly integrations."
- "RentChain's near-term enterprise roadmap focuses on PAD feasibility, vacancy publishing, screening workflows, property manager company operations, and accounting/export readiness."
- "RentChain is not positioned as a full Yardi replacement today."

Unsafe:

- "RentChain replaces Yardi."
- "RentChain is enterprise accounting infrastructure."
- "RentChain automates all screening."
- "RentChain supports PAD today."
- "RentChain provides full enterprise API access today."
- "RentChain is institution-certified."

## Coexistence Strategy

Near-term enterprise adoption should assume coexistence.

RentChain can sit alongside legacy systems by providing:

- vacancy feed/API output
- tenant-facing workflows
- screening request/status workflow
- payment authorization records
- lease/evidence packages
- accounting exports
- audit and compliance records

This reduces migration risk and gives landlords a path to adopt valuable workflows before deciding whether deeper replacement is appropriate.

## Replacement Strategy

Replacement should be long-term and evidence-driven.

RentChain should only consider replacement positioning after it has:

- accounting export and reconciliation maturity
- portfolio reporting maturity
- bulk operations
- multi-entity role governance
- enterprise support and onboarding
- reliable integration surfaces
- mature lease, payment, screening, maintenance, and tenant workflows

Until then, the right positioning is wedge and complement.
